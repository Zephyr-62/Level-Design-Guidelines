### Guidelines, not a manual
These guidelines are not a definitive tool that is to be applied anywhere mindlessly. 
Active thinking and practical experience is required to make good informed decisions and understand how to use these tools to have a successful result.

### Game Genre 
These level design guidelines focus on 3D games with open exploration. 
Even though most of them are compatible with many other genres, e.g. stealth, shooters, metroidvanias, survival, horror, etc., they do not provide a complete set of rules for these genres, since each one of them has specific design needs. 
Two genres particularly affected by this include platformers and racing games, since their gameplay needs are tied to the structure of the level itself.
While the genres this research focuses on use level design as guidance, the latter examples use level design as the scenery where the action takes place, so they are not explicitly accounted for.
However, most of the concepts explained in this work still provide insights that are applicable to all genres.

### Reader's Level of Expertise 
These guidelines are aimed for junior level designers with no previous experience, as every concept is explained in detail. 
However, this text might also be useful for seniors as a toolbox to check from time to time and be reminded of the many design tricks one can use to influence the player's behaviour.
